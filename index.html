Supplement Information for Blood Pressure Support

Grape Seed Extract (GSE): 300–600 mg

Purpose: Provides ACE inhibition and antioxidant support.

Warning: Can interfere with other antioxidants, so it may be best to separate doses to avoid reduced effectiveness.

Beetroot Powder: 10–12 g (or Beet Juice: 500–600 mL)

Purpose: Supplies nitrates to boost nitric oxide (NO) production for improved blood flow.

Note: Consider timing with other nitrate boosters (like Cress Powder).

Cress Powder: 5–7 g

Purpose: Provides additional nitrates for NO production and vascular health.

L-Citrulline: 3 g

Purpose: Different precursor to NO via a different pathway—no need for saliva to convert it.

Note: Great complementary to beetroot for enhancing NO production.

Magnesium: 200–400 mg

Purpose: Supports blood vessel relaxation and cardiovascular health.

Note: Keep an eye on dosage if using with potassium.

Potassium

Purpose: Advisable for most with high blood pressure (if kidney function is normal and no medications are increasing potassium, e.g., ACE inhibitors or diuretics).

Note: Best to get from food sources rather than supplements due to risk of imbalance or overdose.

Garlic (Aged Garlic Extract): 600 mg

Purpose: Mild vasodilator, reduces arterial stiffness, improves gut flora, and enhances vitamin absorption.

Coenzyme Q10 (CoQ10): 100 mg

Purpose: Supports endothelial health and energy production. Often combined with Omega-3 for better effect.

Omega-3 (EPA/DHA): 1,000 mg

Purpose: Anti-inflammatory, supports endothelial function.

Hawthorn Berry: 250–500 mg

Purpose: Promotes nighttime blood vessel relaxation and cardiac support.

Hibiscus Tea: 1–2 cups (optional)

Purpose: Natural ACE inhibition and diuretic effect to support blood pressure.

Note: Optional—ensure no contraindications with current medications.

Folic Acid / Folate

Folic Acid: Synthetic form; 30-40% of people with MTHFR mutations cannot convert it into active methyl folate (5-MTHF). Unconverted folic acid leads to high homocysteine, causing hypertension.

Warning: Excess folic acid can cause depression and psychosis in some individuals.

Best Form: 5-MTHF is the active, bioavailable form of folate.

B Vitamins (Specific Forms)

B2 (Riboflavin-5-phosphate)

Best Form: Riboflavin-5-phosphate (active form)

Purpose: Supports MTHFR enzyme function and nitric oxide production.

Warning: Safe, excess turns urine yellow but causes no toxicity.

Recommended Intake: 1.1–1.3 mg.

B6 (Pyridoxal-5-phosphate / P5P)

Best Form: Pyridoxal-5-phosphate (active form)

Purpose: Reduces homocysteine, supports vascular and neurological health.

Warning: Long-term use (>100 mg/day) can lead to nerve damage (neuropathy).

Recommended Intake: 1.3–2.0 mg.

Upper Limit: 100 mg/day (long-term).

B9 (5-MTHF / L-methylfolate)

Best Form: 5-MTHF (active form)

Purpose: Active folate for methylation, homocysteine reduction—crucial for MTHFR mutations.

Warning: Excess can mask B12 deficiency, potentially worsening neurological symptoms.

Recommended Intake: 400 mcg.

Upper Limit: 1,000 mcg/day (from supplements, not food).

B12 (Methylcobalamin)

Best Form: Methylcobalamin (active form)

Purpose: Reduces homocysteine, supports vascular and nerve function.

Warning: Generally safe, but high doses may overstimulate methylation, leading to anxiety or insomnia.

Recommended Intake: 2.4 mcg.

Upper Limit: No established upper limit (but monitor if sensitive).

Other Vitamins

Vitamin D (D3 / Cholecalciferol)

Best Form: D3 (cholecalciferol)

Purpose: Regulates the renin-angiotensin system, supports endothelial and immune function.

Warning: Excess (>4,000 IU/day long-term) can cause hypercalcemia and kidney strain.

Recommended Intake: 600–800 IU.

Upper Limit: 4,000 IU/day.

Vitamin C (Ascorbic acid or Liposomal C)

Best Form: Ascorbic acid (standard) or liposomal (better absorption).

Purpose: Antioxidant, improves endothelial function, mildly lowers blood pressure.

Warning: Excessive intake (>2,000 mg/day) can cause diarrhea or kidney stone formation in susceptible individuals.

Recommended Intake: 75–90 mg.

Upper Limit: 2,000 mg/day.

Vitamin E (Mixed Tocopherols + Tocotrienols)

Best Form: Mixed tocopherols and tocotrienols

Purpose: Antioxidant, supports vascular health.

Warning: High doses (>400 IU/day) may increase bleeding risk, especially with blood thinners.

Recommended Intake: 15 mg (22.4 IU).

Upper Limit: 1,000 mg (1,500 IU) per day.
